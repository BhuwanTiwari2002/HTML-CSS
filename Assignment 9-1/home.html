About Tesla Charter Enginnering School

Since 2002, Tesla has provided a learning environment that is caring, inspirational, educational and fun. The staff members continually reflect on how to make the Tesla experience rewarding and enjoyable for our students while providing them with the individual attention they need and deserve. And with regular feedback and guidance from the Tesla Charter Board, the staff stays up-to-date with what is evolving in business and industry. All of this adds up to a personal world-class STEM education for all Tesla students.


What is the mission of Tesla Charter Enginnering School

The mission of Tesla Engineering Charter School is to integrate a broad exposure to the field of engineering and technology with a balanced high school experience and to prepare those with the aptitude and passion to pursue post-secondary education and careers in science, technology, engineering, and math. Our partners support and serve our school in a variety of capacities. Most have members of their company or organization serving on the Tesla Charter Board. Why should you choose a charter school?

Charter schools have unique freedom and flexibility not found in public school districts, and their freedom from the red tape of public education often allows them to dedicate increased resources and energy on supporting students in excelling academic standards. A charter school can help a child excel through a more rigorous curriculum. While charter schools have particular freedom in curriculum design and management, they are often held to a higher standard than traditional public schools. Some educational benefit include real world learning, customizied curriculum promotion of Inclusion and Community Involvement and more.


A snapshot of Tesla Charter Enginnering School Facts

  1. Founded in 2002 with the support of the AASD and eight local business partners
  2. Implemented Project Lead The Way curriculum in 2003
  3. Certified in 2006, became the first fully certified PLTW high school in Wisconsin
  4. 2016-17 Enrollment was 130 students
  5. 90+ percent of graduates go on to post-secondary education
  6. Seven staff members - two full time and five part time
  7. Charter board meets monthly to provide ideas, insight and governance
