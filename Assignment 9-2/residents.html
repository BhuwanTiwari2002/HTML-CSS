Education

Menasha offers a wide range of educational opportunities from pre-k to the university level, as well as life long learning opportunities. The Menasha School District's mission is to reach every student every day, offering comprehensive programing that challenges students and prepares them for a wide variety of careers.The district recently received a grade of exceeds expectations on the Wisconsin Department of Public Instruction 2015-6 report cards. Students at Menasha schools have opportunities in the fine arts, athletics, enrched course offiings, bi-lingual education, and strong core offerings integrated with the latest technology.Residents in the City are also offered the choice of a thriving parochial sytem including St. Mary Catholic Schools & Trinity Lutheran School.


Finance & Assessor

Our Finance Department provides for the financial activities of the City including: real and personal property assessment by the City Assessor's Office; management of data and report generation by Information Systems collection and investment of funds received from fees and taxes by the City Treasurer; billing of special assessments and other miscellaneous charges by Accounts Receivable; payment for services and material by Accounts Payable and accounting, debt management, budget preparation, internal control and financial reporting by the City Comptroller. Real Estate taxes can be paid in full on or before January 31st or in four equal installments on or before January 31st, March 31st, May 31st and July 31st. Any special assessments or special charges must be paid in full with the first installment payment. Personal Property taxes must be paid in full on or before January 31st.


Police

Thank you for taking the time to visit us. We are a community focused police department serving a population of over 17,500 people. Our goal is to go beyond traditional police services to make Menasha a safe and desirable place to live, work and visit. Our mission is to "Be a model of modern police services built upon a strong partnership with the community using respect, creativity and technology to find solutions and improve the quality of life and safety for all". We work towards our mission every day by using the Procedural Justice concept of LEED: Listen, Explain, Equity and Dignity. Our goal is to use LEED in every call for service, every traffic stop and every issue that we are asked to assist with.
